Visual Brand Guidelines

Li's Spa — Redmond, WA

Purpose

This guide defines the visual identity, voice, and brand standards for Li's Spa. It ensures consistency across all marketing channels — social media, web, print, signage, and client communications. Every piece of content should feel like it came from the same calm, trusted, professional source.

Foundation

Brand Story & Mission

Li's Spa has been a trusted space for healing, wellness, and personal care in Redmond since 2012. Founded by Runli Li — an experienced massage therapist who believed wellness should always feel personal — the spa was built on care, empathy, and genuine connection with every client.

Under new ownership, Li's Spa carries that same legacy forward with a renewed commitment to elevating the mission. Owner Manisha brings a personal story of transformation — from corporate burnout to discovering the profound power of therapeutic care — that defines the brand's soul.

Mission Statement

"To provide a sanctuary of wellness and healing where every person is cared for with kindness, treated like family, and guided on their personal journey to feeling better — naturally."

Brand Promise

"People before profit. Connection before transaction. Every guest feels safe, valued, and restored — every single time."

Logo

Logo Usage Rules

Consistent logo usage builds recognition and trust.

Clear Space

Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the "L" in "LI'S" on all sides. No text, graphics, or elements should encroach into this zone.

Minimum Size

• Primary horizontal: 120px wide (digital) / 1.5in (print)

• Stacked: 80px wide (digital) / 1in (print)

• Icon mark: 24px (digital) / 0.3in (print)

Approved Uses

✓ On white or cream backgrounds

✓ On dark slate (#2C3E50) backgrounds

✓ On clean photography with sufficient contrast

✓ At approved color variations only

Never Do This

✗ Stretch, skew, or rotate the logo

✗ Change colors outside approved palette

✗ Place on busy or low-contrast backgrounds

✗ Add shadows, glows, outlines, or gradients

✗ Rearrange or separate mark from wordmark

✗ Recreate in a different typeface

Usage Rules

• Deep Slate — primary text/headers. Headlines, navigation, strong statements.

• Sage Green — accent/trust color. CTAs, links, section labels, wellness content.

• Warm Gold — warmth/premium accent. Sparingly for highlights, dividers, featured elements.

• Linen & Cream — background surfaces. Create breathing room and a spa-like feel.

• Never use bright, saturated, or neon colors. The palette is muted, natural, and grounding.

Visual Identity

Typography

Two typefaces that balance elegance with clarity. Premium but approachable.

Display & Headlines

Playfair Display

Headlines, page titles, feature text, pull quotes

Weights: Bold (700) for headlines, Regular (400) for accent

Relax. Rejuvenate. Renew.

Body & Interface

Lato

Body copy, captions, buttons, labels, navigation, social

Weights: 400 (Regular), 700 (Bold), 900 (Black for emphasis)

Your body deserves rest. Your mind deserves quiet. Book your session today.

Fallback Stack

If Playfair Display is unavailable, use Georgia. If Lato is unavailable, use system sans-serif. Never substitute with decorative or script fonts.

Visual Identity

Photography & Visual Style

Every image should feel calm, clean, warm, and authentic. Photography sets the emotional tone before a single word is read.

Do Use

✓ Soft, warm lighting (golden hour, candlelight)

✓ Close-up details: hot stones, oil, towels, candle flames

✓ Muted, earthy tones aligned with the color palette

✓ Real treatment room imagery (not overly staged stock)

✓ Calm faces, closed eyes, relaxed hands

✓ Clean, uncluttered compositions with breathing room

Avoid

✗ Bright, harsh, or fluorescent lighting

✗ Overly saturated or filtered images

✗ Generic stock photos that feel impersonal

✗ Cluttered or messy backgrounds

✗ Text burned into photos (overlay separately)

✗ Anything clinical, medical, or sterile-looking

Instagram Grid Aesthetic

The grid should feel cohesive as a whole — alternating between warm close-up details, clean quote/text graphics on cream backgrounds, and atmospheric spa imagery. Aim for a repeating rhythm: image, graphic, image.
